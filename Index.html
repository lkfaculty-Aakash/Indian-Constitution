üéØ Special Focus: Drafting Committee

The Drafting Committee was established on August 29, 1947 and was the most crucial committee of the Constituent Assembly. Under Dr. B.R. Ambedkar's leadership, this seven-member committee:

  • Published the first draft in February 1948
  • Allowed 8 months for public discussion and feedback
  • Published the revised second draft in October 1948
  • Completed their work in less than 6 months of actual working
  • Sat for only 141 days in total

‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement & Implementation

üóìÔ∏è Why January 26?

January 26 was chosen as the date of commencement because of its historical significance. On this day in 1930, Purna Swaraj (Complete Independence) day was celebrated following the Lahore Session resolution of December 1929 by the Indian National Congress.

üìÖ Phased Implementation

November 26, 1949

‚ö° Immediate Effect Articles

Articles that came into force immediately:

  • Articles 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 - Citizenship
  • Article 60 - President's Oath
  • Article 324 - Elections
  • Articles 366, 367 - Definitions & Interpretations
  • Articles 379, 380, 388 - Provisional Parliament
  • Articles 391, 392, 393 - Transitional Provisions
January 26, 1950

üéâ Full Implementation

The entire Constitution came into effect, replacing:

  • Indian Independence Act of 1947
  • Government of India Act of 1935
  • All related amending acts

Exception: The Abolition of Privy Council Jurisdiction Act (1949) was continued.

üîß Special Provisions

Article 391: Gave the President temporary power to amend the First and Fourth Schedules between the Constitution's passing and commencement. This power was later omitted by the 42nd Amendment Act of 1976 during the Emergency period (1975-77).

üèõÔ∏è Continuity of Governance

The Constituent Assembly didn't end after adopting the Constitution. It continued as the provisional parliament of India from January 26, 1950, until the formation of the new Parliament after the first general elections in 1951-52.
